Extra

  • Bin the data according to the covering to generate a transition probability matrix such that any data outside the computational domain is lumped into a so-called "nirvana" state [4]. Trajectories leaving the domain are reinjected using one of two possible algorithms.
  • Transition path theory statistics [5] are computed on the stationary, time-homogenous Markov chain induced by the transition probability matrix.
  • The outputs are written to a .h5 file.

Prepare the data into one of two formats: .mat or .h5 such that the head of the file contains four variables, x0, xT, y0 and yT. See test/x0x5-NA-undrogued.mat and test/x0x5-NA-undrogued.h5 for example trajectory data from undrogued drifters in the North Atlantic obtained from the NOAA GDP [6].
